Get More From Your System.

Tips and tricks to help you get the most out of your Vivint system. Updated regularly as I learn what works best in the field. Tap any question to expand.
📱 The App

Open the Vivint app → tap the shield icon on the home screen. You'll see three options: Disarmed, Away, and Stay. Away arms all sensors including motion detectors. Stay arms perimeter only — perfect for when you're home at night.

You can also set up automations to arm automatically at a certain time each night.

In the app go to Settings → Users → Add User. You can set different permission levels — Full Control, Arm/Disarm only, or Guest. Each user gets their own login and their own 4-digit panel code.

Tip: Give each person their own unique code so you can track who armed/disarmed and when.

Every motion event from your cameras gets saved as a Clip. Find them under the camera icon → Clips. You can filter by camera or date.

To save a clip to your phone, tap it → tap the download icon. Clips are stored for 30 days by default. Download anything important before then.

📹 Cameras

This is almost always caused by insects flying past the camera at night — they love the infrared glow. A few fixes:

  • Adjust the motion sensitivity down slightly in camera settings
  • Draw a custom activity zone that excludes the area closest to the lens
  • Move the camera if it's pointing at a light source that attracts bugs

In the app, tap a camera → tap the gear icon → Activity Zones. Draw a box around the area you actually care about — your driveway, front door, etc. The camera will only trigger clips when motion happens inside that zone.

This is the single biggest thing you can do to reduce false alerts.

Doorbell camera lag is usually a WiFi signal issue. The doorbell needs a strong 2.4GHz signal — if your router is far away or on 5GHz only, it'll have trouble.

  • Check signal strength in the app under camera settings
  • If below 60%, consider a WiFi extender near the front door
  • Make sure your internet plan is at least 25Mbps upload
🚪 Sensors

Put your system in Test Mode first. In the app: Settings → System → Test Mode. This lets you trigger sensors without setting off the alarm or alerting the monitoring center.

Walk through every door and window you have sensors on. The panel will beep to confirm each sensor is communicating. Test mode automatically disables after 30 minutes.

Most common causes:

  • Pets — if you have animals under 40lbs, adjust sensitivity to Pet Immune mode
  • HVAC vents — hot/cold air blowing across the sensor can trigger it
  • Sunlight — direct afternoon sun moving across the sensor's field of view
  • Curtains — movement near a window can register as motion

Try repositioning the sensor slightly, or contact me and I'll come take a look.

🔒 Smart Home

Yes — in the app go to Automations → Add Rule → Smart Lock. You can set it to lock automatically at a specific time, or trigger it when the system arms in Away mode. I recommend: when system arms Away → lock all doors. You'll never leave the house unlocked again.

Set up a schedule in the app: cooler when you're away, comfortable when you're home. The thermostat can also integrate with the security system — when you arm Away mode, it automatically adjusts to an energy-saving temp.

In Austin summers, I recommend setting away temp to 78-80°F and home temp to 72-74°F. The savings add up fast.
